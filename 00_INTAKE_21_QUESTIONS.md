# BETTERMAN — Intake: The 21 Questions (ANSWERED)
**Project:** Measurable impact on America + local community, 2026–2031. Tracking starts at zero.
**Answered:** 2026-06-11
**Subject:** Mike, 45. Creative director (corporate messaging, digital/copy/events). Wife 46; kids 16, 13, 10. History: Project Angel Food, food delivery, baseball coaching. Annual solo backpacking trips with each kid.

## Ground truth
1. **Location:** Los Angeles area
2. **Hours:** floor 2/wk (busy season), ceiling 10/wk (open weeks) — feast-or-famine pattern; design for bursts
3. **Budget:** under $1k/yr — time, skills, and gear are the contribution, not cash
4. **Non-negotiables:** son's baseball; health routine (workouts, sleep)

## Assets
5. **Activatable network:** musicians & writers; event production world (venues, AV, staging, crews)
6. **Skills he WANTS to give:** photography/documentation; creative direction/messaging; AI/realtime tech (Unreal, Notch, local models). NOT offered: free event production (would resent it)
7. **Render farm compute:** maybe — depends on the use
8. **Physical assets:** camera gear; vehicle/hauling capacity

## Direction
9. **Problems that cut deep:** homelessness; kids without opportunity; loneliness/disconnection
10. **Energized by:** direct service — face-to-face, sees the person
11. **Family:** kids involved where natural (service as imprint, like the backpacking trips)
12. **Build vs join:** start something new, but join/embed first — year 1–2 inside existing orgs, then launch what's missing
13. **Bet size:** ONE BIG BET — everything compounds into a single flagship effort
14. **Civic work:** yes, open to it (school board, committees, leagues)
15. **Mentoring:** both — group workshops AND a few deep one-on-one mentees

## Calibration
16. **Impact metric:** MINDS CHANGED — media/messaging that shifts how people see an issue (corrected from "people helped"; both matter, minds changed is the headline)
17. **2031 sentence:** "Built a storytelling engine" — a program teaching kids/creatives to tell stories that change minds, running without him
18. **Recognition:** invisible is fine — the work is the reward
19. **Burnout triggers:** wasted money on org overhead; orgs as hidden profit channels. → Deep distrust of charity middlemen. Design rule: money/effort goes to the work, not the institution. Radical transparency required.
20. **Commitment ceiling:** "as far as spam no, do I sign petitions, yes" — low-bureaucracy, authentic commitments; no name-lending to machines
21. **Already inside:** kids' schools (×3 parent networks); youth baseball league (coach history + son's team); industry orgs/communities (creative, events, AI/tech)

---

## Synthesis (v1 — the shape of the bet)
The answers converge hard. One candidate flagship:

**A storytelling engine for the unseen of Los Angeles.**
Mike + young creatives he mentors (workshops via schools/league/industry networks) document the people LA looks past — the homeless, the disconnected, the kids without a path — through photography, film, and realtime/AI-augmented media. Direct service IS the act of seeing people and telling their stories. The output changes minds at scale (his actual metric). The engine is the deliverable: by 2031 it runs without him.

Why it fits: uses the skills he wants to give (photo, messaging, AI/tech) and none he'd resent; direct service + mentoring + building, in one; near-zero cash burn (skills > dollars); no charity-middleman overhead to distrust — he IS the infrastructure; kids can be involved naturally; invisible-author model fits ("the subject is the star"); embeds first in existing orgs (year 1–2) before launching.

**Status: PROPOSED — not yet ratified by Mike.**
